 Course Description

Systems of linear equations, matrix theory, real vector spaces, linear transformations, eigenvectors and eigenvalues, inner products, orthogonal transformations, least-squares solutions, applications.

 

Prerequisite

MATH-111 or equivalent or permission of the instructor.

 

 

Text

David Lay, Linear Algebra and its applications, Fourth edition, Pearson, 2012.

Quizzes

We will have 9-11 quizzes, approximately one a week, each Tuesday.  The quizzes will be on the material covered not the most recent Friday, but the Friday 10 days previous as well as the material covered the previous week Monday Thursday. The lowest two will be dropped.  This is for either a poor performance or if you miss.  The quizzes will be problems very similar to or directly taken from the practice homework.

 

 

 

Tests

Three tests will be given in class on Friday September 30, Friday October 28, and Friday December 2.  Make-up tests will only be given under exceptional circumstances (to be documented), and only if you notify me prior to the exam date. If a make-up is not given, your grade for the missed test is zero.

 

 

 

Homework

Homework will be assigned each class and due on Friday at the end of the week. There will be several problems assigned as practice problems (not to hand in), and 2-4 problems assigned each day to be handed in. You are to write neatly and legibly. Write down the statement of the problem followed by the solution. Show all your steps. You are encouraged to work on homework together.

 

 

Final

The final date and time will be posted as soon as it is announced. It is mandatory and comprehensive. Make-up finals will not be given.
